    Best beach destinations in India to visit in monsoon

    1. Goa

    Monsoons unveil a different side of Goa, where you get to see rain-drenched beaches, vegetation-covered land, and lively waterfalls that create a mesmerising ambience.

    During this season, the beaches are peaceful and calming as you don’t see the usual crowd. Also, the accommodations and food are cheaper, which makes the rainy season the perfect time to visit Goa.

    What to do in Goa?

    To enjoy the scenic beauty and nature at its best, you can head to one of the two beautiful islands in Goa, Divar or Chorao.

    The destination is also home to dense tropical forests and wildlife sanctuaries like Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary or the Bondla Wildlife Sanctuary that house the indigenous flora and fauna of Goa.

    During monsoons in Goa, you can enjoy adventures like river cruising and river rafting in the Mandovi River if you want adrenaline-pumping action.

    Who should visit Goa?

    Goa in monsoons is suitable for nature lovers, adventure lovers, and party-goers.

    Which attractions to visit in Goa?

    Visit Dudhsagar waterfalls, check-out spice gardens, go to Aguada fort, and enjoy the Dolphin show for a pleasant travel experience.

    How to reach Goa?

    Catch a flight from Delhi Airport (DEL) to Goa (GOI) and reach your destination in a short duration.

    2. Puducherry

    Monsoons are a perfect time to visit Puducherry because of the colourful and vibrant surroundings. The old and quaint charm of yellow-tinted villas, cobble-stoned streets, old colonial houses, and seafront cafes becomes more inviting when washed with rain.

    The weather gets a little cool during the months between July and September. During this time, Puducherry also enjoys some festivities and celebrations like French National Celebration Day, a perfect occasion to enjoy French cuisine and wine.

    What to do in Puducherry?

    A popular monsoon honeymoon destination in India, Puducherry is home to the Rock Beach. This 1.5 km long beach is one of the few remarkable places to visit in Puducherry. While visiting this beach, you can also check out other landmarks located around the beach, including the Statue of Joan Arc, the French War Memorial, and Heritage Town Hall.

    If you want a religious vibe, the ISKON temple is another must-visit location. Every Sunday people gather here to chant, dance, and enjoy a feast.

    Puducherry is home to an experimental township, Auroville, known as the “City of Dawn.” When visiting this place, you can take a tour of Sanjeev Nagar village, Matrimandir, the local artisan’s place, and the Svaram musical unit to have a good time.

    Who should visit Puducherry?

    Families, couples on honeymoon, nature lovers, and beach lovers.

    Which attractions to visit in Puducherry?

    Visit Sri Aurobindo Ashram, check-out Old Lighthouse, and enjoy food at renowned cafes like Cafe de Flore, Rendezvous Cafe, and Tanto in Puducherry.

    How to reach Puducherry?

    Catch a flight from DEL to Chennai International Airport. Then, book a cab to take you to Puducherry.

    3. Andaman and Nicobar Islands

    Home to exotic plants and animals, Andaman and Nicobar is a group of nearly 570 islands. During the rainy seasons, the lush green vegetation and scenic beauty come to life in Andaman.

    The destination is less crowded in the months between July and September, and the off-season packages can make your trip affordable. During monsoons, the rain-drenched beaches and alluring natural beauty make Andaman and Nicobar islands one the best monsoon destinations in India.

    What to do in Andaman and Nicobar?

    One of the best places to visit in monsoon, Andaman and Nicobar Islands offer a host of adventurous activities, like jet skiing, scuba diving, and snorkelling. These activities are also offered during the rainy season if the downpour is not very heavy.

    Another must-check-out destination in Andaman and Nicobar is the Radhanagar beach with glittering white sand. One of the best beaches in India, the place is full of towering trees and promises breathtaking beauty.

    For history buffs, the Kala Pani jail is an important attraction. Many freedom fighters were once exiled here. If you visit this place, check out the light and sound show offering a glimpse of the Indian freedom struggle.

    Who should visit Andaman and Nicobar?

    Adventure enthusiasts, couples, beach lovers, nature lovers.

    Which attractions to visit in Andaman and Nicobar?

    Havelock Island for adventurous activities, Wandoor Beach for enjoying clear blue water amid lush greenery, and Diglipur for witnessing mud volcanoes.

    How to reach Andaman and Nicobar?

    The fastest and most convenient way to reach Andaman and Nicobar is to board a flight from Delhi Airport to Port Blair.

    4. Mumbai

    Mumbai is one of the best holiday destinations in India during monsoons. Rains in Mumbai put the fast-paced city life on hold and lend a refreshing change to the cityscape. During this weather, the lush greenery, cooler temperatures, and vibrant atmosphere make the city more inviting.

    During monsoons, Mumbai's street food, including bhajia, pakora, vada pav, and chaat, become even more appealing. The rains in Mumbai inject a festive fervour into the streets, which can make your trip worthwhile.

    What to do in Mumbai?

    To make the most of the rainy season in Mumbai, take a stroll at the Marine Drive. During this season, the waves get higher, and the place becomes more livelier. The Worli Sea Face is another place to visit in monsoon in India as it offers picturesque views of the ocean.

    If you do not mind a short drive while in Mumbai, you can also take a one-day trip to Lonavala. A popular hill station, the destination offers a tranquil escape from city life. It is enveloped with lush greenery, which becomes even more charming during the rainy season.

    Your trip to Mumbai during Monsoons cannot be deemed complete without a visit to the Gateway of India. The architectural grandeur shines anew after being washed away by the rains. You can also enjoy the snacks at one of the stalls to make the most of your visit to the Gateway of India.

    Who should visit Mumbai?

    Beach lovers, foodies, history buffs, and nature lovers

    Which attractions to visit in Mumbai?

    Visit Elephanta Caves, explore Sanjay Gandhi National Park, check out Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya, and take a stroll at Juhu Beach.

    How to reach Mumbai?

    You can get a direct flight from Delhi to Mumbai.
